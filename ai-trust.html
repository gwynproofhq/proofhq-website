AI Trust

No silent writeback. No black-box scoring.

PROOF is designed so AI supports the workflow without hiding the reasoning. The system should make inspection more transparent, not less.

Evidence-linked suggestions

Signals and recommendations should point back to the underlying deal context, not appear out of nowhere.

Human review loop

No silent CRM updates

Trust mechanisms

Designed for operational clarity.

  • Evidence-linked outputs instead of unexplained scores
  • Visible override history for managers and leaders
  • Audit trail for operational changes and writeback
  • Human confirmation before sensitive updates
  • Configurable standards instead of one generic model

AI should reduce reconstruction, not replace judgment.

That is the point of PROOF: make deal reviews faster and more defensible while keeping humans in control of the parts that matter.

Evidence source privacy

What PROOF reads — and what it does not.

PROOF connects to CRM, email, and calendar systems to collect evidence signals. Here is exactly what it accesses and what it ignores.

  • CRM: deal fields, stage history, contacts, and activity records
  • Email: metadata only — sender, recipient, timestamps, response frequency. PROOF never reads email content or attachments.
  • Calendar: meeting titles, participant lists, and scheduling patterns. Not meeting notes or recordings.
  • Voice-to-CRM: rep-dictated updates mapped to deal fields. Audio is processed and discarded.
  • Slack and Teams: outbound notifications only. PROOF does not read channel messages or DMs.
