Why PROOF

Most sales playbooks exist. Very few actually run.

Qualification frameworks, stage rules, review questions, and coaching standards usually live in slides, docs, and manager memory. That creates manual prep, inconsistent inspection, and commits driven by confidence instead of proof.

Static playbook

  • Lives in docs and onboarding
  • Applied differently by every manager
  • Creates repeat reviews and process drift
  • Does not hold commit standards in workflow

With PROOF

  • Runs inside the daily workflow
  • Applies one operating standard
  • Surfaces evidence gaps before the review
  • Makes commits easier to validate
What PROOF does

Most tools store the playbook. PROOF makes it executable.

CRMs capture updates. Enablement platforms distribute methodology. Conversation tools capture calls. PROOF connects CRM, email, calendar, and voice data to your playbook — then checks whether the deal actually holds up.

Evidence standards

Define what counts as real proof at each stage and for each methodology lens.

Stage enforcement

Hold progression against evidence and make gaps visible before stage changes happen.

Deal review packs

Pre-assemble review context so managers spend less time reconstructing the deal.

Commit validation

Check whether commit-ready deals meet your standard before they shape the forecast.

Pattern intelligence

Spot recurring failure modes, weak signals, and execution drift across the pipeline.
